## Extraction Behavior

- `noise` classifications produce no candidate signal by default.
- `pain_signal_candidate` maps to `pain_signal`.
- `workaround_signal_candidate` maps to `workaround`.
- `buying_intent_candidate` maps to `buying_intent`.
- `competitor_weakness_candidate` maps to `competitor_weakness`.
- `trend_trigger_candidate` maps to `trend_trigger`.
- `needs_human_review` maps to a low-confidence `needs_human_review` signal instead of being silently dropped.
- `signal_id` is deterministic from `evidence_id` and classification.
- Extraction uses concise normalized title/body text and does not invent facts.

## Measurement Methods Policy

Every candidate signal records explicit measurement methods for:

- `signal_type`
- `pain_summary`
- `target_user`
- `current_workaround`
- `buying_intent_hint`
- `urgency_hint`
- `confidence`

All MVP item 5.2 dimensions are `rule_based`. No field claims live LLM measurement.

## Traceability Behavior

Candidate signals preserve:

- `evidence_id`
- `source_url`
- `source_id`
- `topic_id`
- `query_kind`

The same values are duplicated in the structured `traceability` payload and validated on model roundtrip.
