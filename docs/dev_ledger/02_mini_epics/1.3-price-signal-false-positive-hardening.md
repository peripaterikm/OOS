# 1.3 PriceSignal False-Positive Hardening

## Goal

Reduce false positive price signals where monetary amounts or weak commercial phrases are incorrectly treated as current spend, willingness-to-pay, or budget hints.

## Scope

- Harden deterministic PriceSignal extraction only.
- Filter monetary amounts in tax, deduction, receipt-threshold, IRS, MACRS, depreciation, statutory, and compliance-threshold contexts.
- Prevent `$1.25M` from being truncated into `$1.25` spend.
- Avoid treating weak vendor-commercial language such as free demos, consultations, professional training, and technical assistance as willingness-to-pay.
- Preserve valid organic affordability complaints such as `can't afford`, `too expensive`, and `overpriced`.

## Ground Truth Used

- `examples/first_real_open_source_signal_run_v1/founder_manual_labels.json`
- `examples/first_real_open_source_signal_run_v1/founder_manual_labels.md`

## First-Run Examples Covered

- `raw_github_issue_194268452` - `$75` receipt/recovery threshold is not current spend.
- `raw_hn_46725518` - `$1.25M` Section 179 deduction limit is not fake spend.
- `raw_github_issue_4369704245` - `affordable pricing`, free demo, consultation, and training language do not create a meaningful boost.
- `raw_github_issue_4058309053` - generic tax obligations / accurate records copy does not create WTP.
- `raw_hn_47009152` - `can't afford a full time software developer` remains a valid affordability complaint.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [x] Receipt thresholds are not extracted as current spend.
- [x] Tax/deduction limits are not truncated into fake spend hints.
- [x] Generic `affordable pricing` remains low-confidence or filtered and does not imply budget.
- [x] Explicit spend and price complaints still extract correctly.
- [x] Evidence citation remains required for extracted signals.
- [x] Full validation passes.
